I think somewhere around the halfway mark of Object Oriented Ruby I realized I was not learning anything, I was just going through the motions.  I would finish a lab, and then stop, look back at what I had done, and invariably forget how it was that I got there.  I started to doubt that I was learning anything at all, that I was just regurgitating information that I found in a lesson, following a blueprint for success I could not achieve without instruction.  It is easy to be discouraged at this point, but I don't have that luxury.  I can't look back at my path and say I wasn't progressing.  I... must... move... forward.

So, here I am, trudging through the depths of what it means to build advanced class methods, to create something out of nothing and call it a thing.  That's kind of where it hit me, I'm not failing as long as I'm moving forward.  As slow as it may seem at times, eventually it gets into your skull.

As someone who has not "figured it out" completely, I can say to those just starting out, it will get worse, but maybe that's better.
