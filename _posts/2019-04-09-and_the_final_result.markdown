Well, here we are.  I've been working on building my abilities to include a full stack, and the time has come to put all of that together.  It's been a long journey, one that I am happy to be concluding as I begin to finally see how all of these wonderful concepts fit together.  My Portfolio project has been created, and will soon be destroyed and rebuilt using React Hooks, the new hotness, but for the time being it is a great time to look at what I can do.

I remember when I was still learning basic ruby concepts I would look at what I had done, and think, 'How do I make this something I can use?'.  It was difficult to think about how much trouble I was having internalizing OOP concepts and making its principles my own.  I've always been impressed with clean code, obsessed with it actually.  While I was going through this Learn.co course, for a time I was working for a small company building websites for credit unions. Shout out to all my Omnicommandos!  While there I started noticing habits that I had formed during my studies, and began to eliminate the bad ones, while building the good ones.  I now understand how much I crave simplicity in my code.  All too often I see programmers using crazy amounts of code to build the simplest tasks.  Strip it down, understand what you're building, understand its purpose, and don't overextend.  That is my philosophy, and thus far it has served me well.

I've begun to look at my code and think of ways to simplify.  If it becomes too convoluted, I often will destroy what I've built with a clearer view of what I'm trying to do, and build it with a renewed sense of purpose.  I no longer fear destroying work I've done, because with Ruby and React, the convention is all there, I know what needs to be done, and I build according to plan.
