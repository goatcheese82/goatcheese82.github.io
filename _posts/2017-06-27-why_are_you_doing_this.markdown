title:  "Why Are You Doing This?"
date:   2017-06-27 18:34:30 +0000
---

This is a question you need to ask yourself not only before you begin, but the entire time you're progressing.  If you lose sight of what's important, the purpose, you might as well slap failure on your forehead.

I've got a friend who recently applied and was accepted to a bootcamp, and was very excited to start.  He's been working as a laborer and was ready to start learning to do something different.  I asked him how he was preparing, and he told me he was reading a few online tutorials, and doing a few lessons on codecademy.  While I always want the best for my friends, I remember warning him that he needed to do a lot more preparation than he thought he did.  You must live it, you must breathe it.  It has to be the reason you wake up.

When that same friend came to me and told me he had returned from the bootcamp and was not going back only two weeks later, I began to wonder why.  He wanted a change just as I did, and do.  When I asked him why, he told me "It's just not for me, I gave it a shot, and I didn't like it".  Interesting, I thought.  He didn't quit because it was too hard, he quit because he didn't know he was doing something that he wanted to do.  He didn't didn't want to change his view, he wanted to change his career.

Don't look at coding as a change in career.  You must look at it as a change in lifestyle.  It has to encompass your thoughts.  I dream about coding.  I find myself in sticky situations often as I sleep, only to be freed from the clutches of dispair by a well thought out .method I had been playing with earlier in the day.  This is the difference between washing out and continuing.  Don't try it.  Live it.
