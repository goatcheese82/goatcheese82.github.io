After playing with about three hundred ideas and scrapping a thousand of them, I settled on an app that would help me in my daily life.  I have the hardest time making decisions about what to eat.  I'll  buy a car at the drop of a hat, but don't ask me what I'm eating for lunch.

My gastronomical indecision gave rise to the necessity of a meal randomizer which I created as a CLI app.  At first the going was rough.  I had an idea, but I had no idea where to start.  I started by building an app that required way more complicated code than I was ready to write.  Being the stubborn person I am, I decided to push through the craziness that I was slowly creating, and kept digging myself deeper and deeper into this strange abyss that I couldn't recognize even if I wanted.  I started to get pretty frustrated at this point.

Having blasted through OOP and assuming I had a pretty good grasp on the concepts, I felt it still might be good to give the previous sections of instruction a quick repass.  I started to review the concepts that were giving me trouble and it all seemed very familiar.  This didn't help in my plight, and I began to feel I might be in a bigger hole than I thought.  It was scary and frustrating, and about this time I just went and got a job.

After working for a company building credit union websites for about a month, I realized just how much I really wanted to add to my knowledge base.  I had been picking at my app on a daily basis, but didn't  have the time to really dig into it, so by the time I returned to plucking keys for real, I found that I had completely forgotten two things.
     1.  The concepts I worked so hard to learn.
     2.  Whatever the hell it was I was trying to do with this app.
     3.  
Upon returning to the app it seemed completely foreign.

Realizing I had no clue what I had done, I decided it was time for a one on one with a coach.  If you have never had a one on one, and you have that ability, I highly recommend you do next time you feel stuck.  I set up a session with Enoch, and kept hacking at it.  When I Enoch looked at my app I think he was even more confused than I was.  It was a mess.  He then gave me the best advice he could have.  I needed to code to the requirements.  I was so enthused at the idea I was creating that I had deviated from what I  was supposed to do.

After taking a bit of additional advice, I started my app all over again, this time based on the requirements I had been given.  What had taken me a month and a half to figure out took me a little over an hour to code after I realized I needed to simplify.  I'm now sitting here with an app I'm happy with.
