I've been working in technology for a long time.  I started building small websites in 2001, and just kind of dabbled.  In 2005 I built my first real website, but it still didn't seem like it was what I want to do.  In 2008 I decided to actually start working and making some money on my abilities in technology.  I got a job with a small development company through my cousin, and was just manipulating some CSS and HTML.  I started dabbling a bit server side while there, and quickly rose up the ranks.  I decided that I would try my hand at hardware at that point, and started working the IT side of things.
For a few years I decided that IT was my calling.  I was having fun doing what I knew, and I loved it.  Something was missing, though, and I started to realize that I need to create.  It was then that I decided that I was fooling myself.  I want to code.  I started looking for schools, and brushing up on what I already knew, but quickly realized how much the landscape has changed.  I needed to find a way to get back in the coding game, and I started preparing to go to Hack Reactor this Winter.  About midway through the Hack Reactor prep, I read about Flatiron, and started taking their prep.  I enjoyed it so much I decided to apply.
Being a Military spouse I applied for a scholarship, and after completing the prep, I was accepted to Flatiron.  I was ecstatic, and decided to start right away.  I quit my IT job at the school, and began my journey.
