title:  "Imitation is the Sincerest Form of Flattery"
date:   2017-08-23 17:27:33 +0000
---


As languages go Ruby is among those I've found easiest to understand.  It's clean, it's intuitive.  It took me 4 months to learn enough Spanish to have a conversation with native speakers.  I found that it was easiest to understand the language by speaking it, and easiest to learn to speak by hearing and reading it.  This makes the learning process cyclical.  I've found this applies not just to spoken languages, but those we use to communicate with machines as well.

So, the way I've started learning to write better code, is by reading better code.  It not only helps me gain a better understanding of syntax, but also gives me ideas of how to construct better programs.  It helps me get ideas to better perfect my art.  If I can emulate the habits of great programmers, I can myself become a great programmer.
